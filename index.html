About the Author

Andy Weir spent two decades writing software, not novels — a habit that still shows. Every book starts from a real equation: how much a potato crop needs to keep one person alive, how a habitat holds pressure, how long a signal takes to cross a gap between planets. The story gets built on top of the math, not the other way around.

He fell into science fiction early, working through the genre's classics as a kid, and spent years posting stories to his own website for free before anyone in publishing noticed. The Martian changed that overnight — a self-published serial that reader demand pushed onto Amazon's bestseller list, then onto The New York Times', then onto a movie screen.

Outside the writing, he's the kind of person who will happily explain delta-v budgets or the history of the space race to anyone who asks — and, by most accounts, mixes a genuinely good drink while he does it. He lives in California.
