Andy Weir built a career as a software programmer for companies including AOL, Palm, MobileIron, and Sandia National Laboratories. A devoted hobbyist of subjects like relativistic physics, orbital mechanics, and the history of manned spaceflight, he began writing The Martian as a free serial on his website at the urging of his fans.

After reader demand, he published the full novel on Amazon's Kindle store in 2011 at the lowest possible price: 99 cents. It became an instant bestseller, climbing to #1 on Amazon's science fiction list within weeks. Crown Publishing picked it up for a major publishing deal, and it was made into a blockbuster film directed by Ridley Scott, starring Matt Damon.

His approach to fiction is deeply scientific: he researches every technical detail — orbital mechanics, atmospheric chemistry, agricultural botany, and astrobiology — so that readers can trust that his characters are solving real problems with real science.

He writes what readers call 'competence porn': smart people solving hard problems with engineering and grit. His subsequent novels and the graphic novel Cheshire Crossing continue this tradition of combining humor, suspense, and iron-clad scientific rigor.
